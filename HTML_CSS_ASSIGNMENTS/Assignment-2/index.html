Bold and Strong:

The b tag is used to make text appear Bold without implying any extra importance or emphasis on the content. It's a purely visual effect, meant to draw attention to certain words or phrases.

The strong tag not only makes text bold, but it also semantically emphasizes the importance of the content. Text wrapped in a strong tag is typically considered more meaningful or important. It conveys that the content has a strong relevance or seriousness in the context.

Italic and Emphasis:

The i tag is used to make text appear in italic style. It is primarily used for stylistic purposes , like denoting foreign words, names, or technical terms, without implying any extra emphasis or importance. It is a purely visual effect.

The em tag not only renders text in italic style but also conveys emphasis or stress on the text's meaning. This tag is semantically stronger and indicates that the content enclosed has greater importance or should be read with emphasis.

Citatons:

In HTML, the cite tag is used to mark up the title of a creative work, such as a book, article, movie, song, or any other reference. It is typically used when citing sources in a document or attributing authorship. The text within the cite tag is rendered in italic by default, indicating that it's a title or reference.

Definitions:

In HTML, the dfn (definition) tag is used to define a term or phrase that is being introduced for the first time within a document. Typically, the first occurrence of the term is marked using the dfn tag, and the definition of the term is usually provided in the same sentence or paragraph.
